## PKM-RSH Research Funding Grantee

**"Network Governance in Supporting the Subjective Well-being of Juvenile Inmates in LPKA"**

We saw hope for the subjective well-being of children behind the cold walls of LPKA (Special Correctional Institution for Children). We aimed to accommodate this hope by managing the collaboration conducted by LPKA Class II Jakarta.

This research used **social network analysis** and **single case study** data analysis techniques, drawing on institutional site analysis, interviews, and observations. Key findings showed that LPKA still lacks comprehensive standard operating procedures (SOPs).

Therefore, the best way for LPKA to accommodate subjective well-being is to develop SOPs for each program, project, and policy — a recommendation directly submitted to the Ministry of Law and Human Rights.

## PKM-AI Research Incentive Awardee

**"Comprehensive Analysis of Public Trust Levels Among Young Individuals in the JakLapor Feature"**

We noticed concerns of data breach among young people using the JakLapor feature operated by the DKI Jakarta Provincial Government. To describe these concerns, we analyzed public trust (PT) variables across various demographics.

We employed:
- **Comparative studies** using Mann-Whitney U and Kruskal-Wallis tests
- **Associative studies** using Spearman correlation
- Questionnaires across demographics of gender, region, generation Y/Z, and education

The results showed that a significant difference exists in public trust between generations Y and Z, with **Generation Z having lower trust**. Consequently, the DKI Jakarta provincial government should conduct a public needs survey for the JakLapor feature specifically targeting Generation Z.

## 1st Place for Paper and 1st Place for Presentation — National Conference

**"Simplification of Limited Liability Company Registration Procedures Through Notaries"**
*Temu Administrator Muda Indonesia 2024*

![Award ceremony at Temu AdMI 2024](images/activity-indonesia-emas-2.png)

We observed the procedural, time, and cost burdens faced by business founders in Indonesia. Using **independent t-test analysis** and **multiple case studies**, we conducted a holistic comparison between countries that use notarization processes and those that don't.

The results showed that countries not using notarization processes experience significantly lower administrative burdens. We recommended integrating or eliminating the role of notaries in the company registration process — a recommendation targeted at the Ministry of Investment/BKPM.

We learned that, perhaps, no public policy can benefit all parties. And that is precisely why the work of analysis matters.
