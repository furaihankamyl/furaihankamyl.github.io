**Category:** Collaboration | **Year:** 2024–2025

Indonesia has hundreds of living languages, millions of daily text conversations in slang and regional dialects, and a digital economy that runs on informal communication. Yet most large language models are trained predominantly on English-language data. When they do support Indonesian, they often miss the texture of how people actually speak.

Sahabat-AI was GoTo's answer to that gap.

## What is Sahabat-AI?

Sahabat-AI is an open-source Large Language Model (LLM) developed by PT GoTo Gojek Tokopedia Tbk in collaboration with PT Indosat Ooredoo Hutchison. It is trained specifically for Bahasa Indonesia and regional languages, currently supporting **Indonesian, Sundanese, Javanese, Batak, and Balinese**.

The model was built with a clear premise: Indonesia's AI future should be shaped by Indonesian language and context, not retrofitted from Western models. Its development involved local universities, media groups, language communities, and global research partners including AI Singapore.

## University Partnerships: My Role

My direct responsibility in this initiative was leading the university collaboration program — building and managing partnerships with institutions that contributed training data, research capacity, and digital talent to the project.

The universities involved included:

* Universitas Indonesia (UI)
* Universitas Gadjah Mada (UGM)
* Institut Pertanian Bogor (IPB)
* Institut Teknologi Bandung (ITB)
* Universitas Sumatera Utara (USU)
* Universitas Hasanuddin (Unhas)
* Universitas Udayana

Each partnership was structured around several forms of collaboration: **training data contribution** by faculty and students, **student internship programs** for AI data training work, **knowledge-sharing sessions** paired with MOU or PKS signings, and in the case of Universitas Indonesia, an **AI research grant** program.

What made this work operationally complex was the end-to-end nature of each engagement, spanning account management, negotiation, and event project management. The latter taught me the technical nuances of event organization, such as invitation management, university crowd coordination, student-and-faculty session design, and high hospitality standards. Every touchpoint needed to reflect both GoTo's institutional standing and the genuine value of the partnership for the universities.

## The Language Community Innovation: Lentera App

<p>
  <img src="/images/images/sahabat-ai-2.jpg" alt="Poster of GoTo-Lentera App essay competition" style="width: 220px !important; float: right; margin-left: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; height: auto;">
  One of the more unconventional parts of Sahabat-AI's data strategy involved a collaboration with <strong>Lentera App</strong>, a local literary platform, to collect informal Indonesian language data.
</p>

The problem we were solving is straightforward but underappreciated: slang, abbreviations, and natural conversational Indonesian are extremely difficult to source from existing publications and academic literature. Phrases like *"Kalo gw liat-liat, si. Gk ada, ya."* or *"Gak begitu, njir."* — the kind of language that fills every Indonesian's text messages, simply don't appear in formal corpora.

The solution was a **writing competition** with Lentera App focused on urban short stories written in conversational, dialogue-heavy language. Participants submitted essays that naturally produced the kind of informal, textured Indonesian that Sahabat-AI needed for training. GoTo served as a supporter, and the collected works, with creator consent, formed part of the training dataset.

This was, to my knowledge, one of the more creative approaches to the data sourcing problem in Indonesian AI development.

## Beyond the Partnership Work: Policy and Compliance

As a PPGR professional embedded in this initiative, my responsibility extended beyond relationship management into **policy compliance**. Specifically, ensuring that all training data used in Sahabat-AI's development did not infringe on intellectual property rights.

This meant working through the legal frameworks around data consent, verifying that university and community contributions were either rights-free, openly licensed, or explicitly consented to by creators. In an ecosystem where AI training data ethics is still an evolving conversation, building this into the process from the start was both a legal necessity and, I think, the right way to build something meant to serve Indonesian society.

## What This Initiative Means

Sahabat-AI is not just a technical product. It is an argument that Indonesian language, regional diversity, and local talent are sufficient and necessary inputs for building AI that actually works for Indonesia. Everything that our team built in Sahabat-AI was a small piece of infrastructure for a larger vision that **Indonesia's AI sovereignty should be built from within.**

I'm glad to have been part of building that foundation.
